Solaris - Our Solar System

Uranus, the seventh planet from the Sun,
is the third largest in our solar system,
measuring four times wider than Earth.
This planet is enveloped in a chilly and windy atmosphere
and features faint rings and over two dozen small moons.
Its distinct nearly 90-degree axial tilt causes it to rotate on its side,
making it appear to spin horizontally. Uranus's blue-green hue is due to the presence of methane,
which absorbs red light and reflects blue, giving the planet its striking color.
